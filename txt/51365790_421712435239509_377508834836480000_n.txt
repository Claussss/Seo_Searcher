relative clauses ( parts ) - prepo. sitions + whom / which in 'extra information clauses ( type 2) you can use a preposition before whom ( for people) and which ( for things): to whom / with whom / about which etc. e. g. 1) sheila, for whom we had been waiting, finally arrived. 2) fortunately we had a map, without which we would have got lost. in spoken english we often keep the pre- position after the verb in the relative clause and we normally use who ( not "whom'). e.g. 1) sheila, who we had been waiting for, finally arrived. 2) yesterday we visited the city museum, which i had never been to before.  2019-01
