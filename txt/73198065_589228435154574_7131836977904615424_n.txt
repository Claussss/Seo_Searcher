using infinitives with too" and "enough" " 1. too + adjective + (for someone)+ + infinitive infinitives often follow expressions with too. too comes in front of an adjective. in the speaker's mind, the use of too implies a negative result. compare: 1 the box is too heavy ( for me) to lift ( i can't lift it ). 2) the box is very heavy ( but i can lift it). 2. enough + noun + infinitive e. g. i don't have enough money to buy this 3. adjective + enough+infinitive c.q. i'm not strong enough to lift a refrigerator.
